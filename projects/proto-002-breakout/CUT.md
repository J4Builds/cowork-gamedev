- Row-pitched brick audio (Atari original scheme: pitch correlates with row) — replaced with chain-index pentatonic on first juice-pass playtest — 2026-05-11. Ascending phrases during tunnel chain-clears felt great, but the same scheme played DESCENDING phrases when the ball cleared rows top-down (the peak moment), and descending sequences registered as disruptive even with in-tune notes. Chain-index pitch (every consecutive brick within timeout = next ascending note, paddle hit resets) makes the peak moment always-triumphant regardless of which physical rows are being hit. Cost: lose row identification in audio; visual color already encodes it, so no real loss.
